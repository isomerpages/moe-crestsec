<h4>Secondary 3 – Restore: Rising Beyond Limits</h4>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">
<img style="width: 100%" height="auto" width="100%" alt="" src="/images/SDT_Sec3_1.jpg">
</div>
<p>As our Secondary Three students step into upper secondary, they face new
challenges and expectations. Restoring confidence means adopting a growth
mindset, staying resilient when challenges arise, and supporting one another
through adversity. By rising beyond limits, they discover their potential
and emerge stronger, ready for greater achievements ahead.</p>
<p></p>
<p><strong><u>MOE-OBS Challenge Camp</u></strong>
</p>
<p>The MOE-OBS Challenge (MOC) Programme, under the National Outdoor Adventure
Education Masterplan, provides Secondary Three students with authentic
outdoor experiences that built confidence, resilience, leadership, and
social awareness while forming strong bonds with peers. Crest has embarked
on the MOC journey from 2026 onwards. Through challenging expeditions,
students step out of their comfort zones to become adventurous, benevolent,
and charismatic individuals.</p>

<p>The IgnITE Skills Challenge, organized by ITE since 2016, is more than
just a competition. It is an invaluable opportunity for students to learn
and then to apply their knowledge and skills in real-world scenarios. Riding
on the level theme of ‘rising beyond limits’, the entire 2025 Secondary
3 cohort form teams and take part in the competition, under the guidance
of our teachers. Through this competition, Crestans discover their interests,
build confidence, develop communication and presentation skills, and gain
valuable insights into various technical and vocational fields. The experience
also nurtures resilience, innovation, and a growth mindset — key traits
that prepare Crestans for future education, careers, and lifelong learning.</p>

<p>Crest adopts an unique internship experience known as the Industrial Experience
Programme (IEP). The IEP equips all Secondary Three Crestans with valuable
work experience through a four weeks internship with the real-world workplaces.
It allows them to explore different careers, apply classroom knowledge
in practical settings, and develop essential skills such as communication,
teamwork, and problem-solving. Through hands-on experience, students learn
responsibility, build confidence, and gain independence.</p>

<p>The Crest curriculum plans authentic learning journeys that aim to extend
learning beyond the classroom by helping students see the real-world relevance
of what they study. Through authentic and hands-on experiences, students
apply subject knowledge in meaningful contexts, strengthening their communication,
problem-solving, and critical thinking skills. These journeys also encourage
collaboration, reflection, and appreciation of how languages and mathematics
shape daily life, culture, and society, making learning more engaging and
purposeful.</p>
